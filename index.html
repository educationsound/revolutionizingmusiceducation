About the Study

This study examines how the historical dominance of Western classical music in education has shaped opportunities and challenges for students from diverse cultural backgrounds. By focusing on the intersections of music, race, society, and culture, it reveals new possibilities for creating a more inclusive and transformative approach to music education.

Download the Dissertation

Key Findings

  • Music education must center students' cultural wealth and lived experiences.
  • Culturally relevant pedagogy fosters identity and empowerment.
  • Western classical dominance perpetuates systemic barriers in education.

About the Author

Dr. Jan-Mitchell Ko is an educator and critical scholar passionate about transforming music education through social justice and cultural relevance. His groundbreaking dissertation redefines music education as a tool for liberation and equity.
